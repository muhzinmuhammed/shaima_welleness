Additional Therapeutic Services

Moxibustion

Traditional Chinese therapy using heated mugwort to warm acupuncture points, promoting circulation and strengthening the body's energy.

Cupping Therapy

Ancient technique using suction cups to improve blood flow, reduce muscle tension, and promote healing in targeted areas.

Electroacupuncture

Enhanced acupuncture treatment using mild electrical stimulation to amplify therapeutic effects and accelerate healing.

Herbal Medicine

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about acupuncture treatment

Most people experience little to no discomfort during acupuncture. The needles are very thin, about the width of a human hair, and insertion is typically painless. You may feel a mild sensation or tingling, which is normal and indicates the treatment is working.

The number of treatments varies depending on your condition, its severity, and how long you've had it. Acute conditions may improve in 3-5 sessions, while chronic conditions typically require 8-12 treatments. Your practitioner will discuss a personalized treatment plan with you.

Yes, we use only sterile, single-use, disposable needles that are discarded after each treatment. All our practitioners follow strict safety protocols and maintain the highest standards of cleanliness and hygiene.

After treatment, you may feel relaxed, energized, or slightly tired. Some people experience immediate improvement, while others notice gradual changes over several sessions. It's normal to feel emotional releases or heightened awareness of your body's sensations.

Many insurance plans now cover acupuncture for certain conditions. We recommend checking with your insurance provider to understand your coverage. We also accept Health Savings Account (HSA) and Flexible Spending Account (FSA) payments.
